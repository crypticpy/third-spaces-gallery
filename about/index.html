About Third Spaces

A City of Austin initiative to help youth discover and connect with third places in their community.

What is Third Spaces?

Third Spaces is an app being developed by the City of Austin's Resident Data Impact Project (RIDP) to help young people discover "third places" ‚Äî spaces beyond home and school where they can hang out, learn, play, and connect with their community.

Think libraries, community centers, parks, rec centers, maker spaces, and more. These are places where Austin youth can find activities, programs, and spaces that match their interests.

What is this Gallery?

This is the Youth Design Gallery ‚Äî a showcase of real app ideas created by Austin students through the Create to Code Challenge.

Students from elementary through high school teamed up to imagine what the Third Spaces app should look like and what features would matter most to them. Now we need your help! Vote for the ideas you love, leave feedback, and help the City of Austin understand what features young people actually want.

Your votes directly shape what gets built. The most popular ideas and features will inform the design of the real Third Spaces app.

How These Designs Were Made

Every design you see here started as a hand-drawn sketch, a written idea, or a concept pitched by a student team. Here's how we brought their visions to life:

1

Students Dream It Up

Teams of Austin students brainstormed, sketched, and described their ideal app features ‚Äî everything from how to find the best hangout spots to wristband check-ins and AI recommendations.

2

Our Team Builds the Mockups

The City of Austin's Create to Code team used AI design tools (like Google Gemini and ChatGPT) to transform those student sketches and ideas into full app screen designs. Think of it like a professional design studio turning rough blueprints into polished visuals.

3

Students Give Feedback

We brought the mockups back to each team for a feedback round. Students told us what looked right, what felt off, and what needed changing ‚Äî just like working with a real design team.

The result? The designs you see here are a true collaboration between student creativity and modern design tools. The ideas, features, and vision all come from Austin youth.

Some designs are based on imagined physical spaces rather than app screens. For those, our team captured what students loved about their "dream third space" and translated those ideas into features that could live inside a real app.

About RIDP

The Resident Data Impact Project (RIDP) is an initiative by the City of Austin that focuses on using technology and data to improve residents' lives while ensuring their privacy and input are respected.

Third Spaces is one of RIDP's key projects, developed with extensive input from Austin youth through workshops, the Youth Council, and initiatives like the Create to Code Challenge.
