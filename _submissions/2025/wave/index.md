Wave is a social discovery platform designed by high schoolers at Austin High School. It helps teens find and share local third spaces ‚Äî libraries, cafes, makerspaces, and creative hubs ‚Äî in downtown Austin. Unlike generic map apps that treat every business the same, Wave puts smaller, independent local spots front and center, surfacing the hidden gems that big platforms overlook.

## What Makes Wave Different

Most third space apps are utility tools: search, find, go. Wave flips that by making social sharing and peer recommendations the core experience. When you open the app, you see what your friends checked into this week, which spots are trending among Austin teens, and honest reviews from people your age. The map is the organizing structure, but community is the engine. Vibe-based filters like "Chill," "Creative," and "Study" let you search by mood instead of keyword, and amenity tags ‚Äî free Wi-Fi, pet-friendly, LGBTQ+ friendly, accessible ‚Äî help you find spaces that actually fit your needs.

## Built By Teens, For Teens

Wave is not just another app with a youth-friendly paint job. It was conceived, designed, and shaped by high schoolers who know firsthand what their peers want: autonomy, authenticity, and places where they can be themselves. The "Submit a Space" feature means the community keeps growing as students discover new spots, and the multi-modal navigation ‚Äî with walk, bike, bus, and drive options ‚Äî reflects the reality that most high schoolers rely on transit, not cars. Wave bridges the gap between the online social life teens already have and the real-world community they are looking for.
